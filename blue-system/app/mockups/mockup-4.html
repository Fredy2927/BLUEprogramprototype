What is B.L.U.E.?

A free encyclopedia of practical skills. Every concept is explained at four difficulty levels so you can read the version that matches what you already know.

How does this site work?

Beginner For anyone, no background needed

It's a website with lots of how-to guides. People write them. Other people check them. Then you can read them for free.

School level If you've finished school

B.L.U.E. is a community-built encyclopedia that teaches practical, step-by-step skills. Anyone can write a lesson. Before it goes up, five people who know the subject read it and vote on whether it's good enough.

Hobbyist If you do this kind of thing for fun

Lessons are organised into a strict prerequisite hierarchy (Levels 1–4). Each lesson has alternate "methods" the community can propose and vote on. A jury of randomly-selected verifiers reviews submissions; majority decision publishes, with mandatory written justification.

Specialist If you build this kind of system

Distributed editorial governance via odd-cardinality jury (default n=5, scaled by RFC-003), reputation-weighted verifier selection, CC BY-SA 4.0 content licence, cross-niche conflict resolution via fork/spin-off, architecture decisions ratified via two-week supermajority RFC vote, monetisation via topically-scoped affiliate links only.

Construction: how a lesson is born

The author writes the lesson and declares its prerequisites, then submits it for verification. A jury of verifiers in the relevant subject is chosen at random. Each verifier must vote with a written justification. Once a majority is reached, the lesson is either published or returned to the author for revision.

prerequisites
The easier lessons a reader should already understand before starting this one. In plain words: "What you need to learn first."
verification
The community process of checking a new lesson is correct and clear. In plain words: "Other people read it and decide if it's good."
jury
A small group (usually five) of randomly-selected verifiers who vote on a single submission. In plain words: "A team picked at random, like at a courtroom."
