How to build a tiny switch that powers every computer

A weekend project, a wafer of silicon, and three terminals: the lesson that opens our hierarchy explains the most important invention of the twentieth century — and asks you to make one yourself.

By @kira and @otto  ·  Published 90 days ago  ·  Beginner 8 min read  ·  1,247 readers found this helpful

⚡ Quick read · 30 seconds A bipolar junction transistor (BJT) is a small switch made from doped silicon. It is the foundational building block of digital electronics. This lesson walks you through fabricating one yourself using the classical diffused-base method.
🌱 Never built anything like this? Read this first. A transistor is a tiny part — smaller than a grain of rice — that decides whether electricity is allowed to flow or not. Your phone has about 16 billion of them inside it. This lesson teaches you to build one, by hand, over a weekend. You don't need an engineering degree, but you'll need patience and a quiet, clean workspace.
