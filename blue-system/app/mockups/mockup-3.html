How it works

In one sentence

Anyone can write a lesson, but it only goes up if 5 people who know the topic agree it is clear and correct.

1. Pick a lesson and read

All lessons are sorted by how hard they are: easy, medium, harder, expert. Hard lessons tell you which easy ones to read first.

2. Or write your own

If you know how to do something well, write a lesson about it. You don't need to be an expert. You just need to be clear.

3. Or help check what others write

If you know a topic well, you can apply to be a "checker". When someone writes a new lesson in your topic, you read it and vote on whether it is ready to be published. You must explain your vote in writing.

What this means in plain English Lessons get checked by real people who know the subject. Not by a computer. Not by one boss. By a small group, the way a jury works.
