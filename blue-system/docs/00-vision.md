# 00 — Vision & Non-Negotiables

## Mission
A single free website where anyone can learn **how to do anything** — from soldering a transistor to building a car engine to practicing a medical procedure — through structured, progressive, community-written guides.

## The one-sentence pitch
> Wikipedia gives you facts. WikiHow gives you trivia. Khan Academy gives you a few subjects. **B.L.U.E. gives you the full skill, from zero knowledge to the edge of human practice — for free.**

## Non-negotiables (from Sebastian's video)
These are the things that, if broken, kill the project:

1. **Free and universally accessible.** No paywalls. No "premium tier."
2. **Profit is not the optimization target.** Money exists only to keep the lights on.
3. **Hierarchy of knowledge is mandatory.** Every Level N guide must be completable using only Level 1…N−1 content.
4. **One canonical guide per topic.** Improvements happen through edits or replacement, not duplication.
5. **No centralized editorial control.** Verifiers are randomly selected per submission, like a jury.
6. **Verifiers must justify their votes.** No silent rejections. Feedback loop is mandatory.
7. **Ads, if any, must be topically relevant and confined.** No banners. No interstitials. No tracking-driven ads.
8. **Resilient to legal/corporate pressure.** Pick jurisdiction and corporate structure with this in mind from day one.

## What we are explicitly NOT building
- A social network
- An AI chatbot (AI may *assist* writers/verifiers, but never replace them)
- A credentialing/certificate business
- A paid course marketplace
- A "creator economy" platform with revenue-share
